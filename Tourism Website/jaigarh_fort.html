Jaigarh Fort

            
             Jaigarh Fort, located on the Aravalli hills above the Amer Fort in Jaipur, Rajasthan, India,
             is a majestic fort renowned for its architecture, cannons, and military history. The fort 
             was built by Jai Singh II in 1726 to protect Amer Fort and its palace complex and was named 
             after him.



             The fort is also known for housing the world's largest cannon on wheels, the Jaivana Cannon. 
             The rugged charm of the fort is enhanced by its sturdy walls and a large central watchtower 
             that offers a panoramic view of the surrounding landscape. The fort's historical significance
             and its impressive engineering make it a must-visit destination in Jaipur.

Architecture and Features

            
    Jaigarh Fort is an outstanding example of the military architecture of its time. The fort features 
    massive walls built with red sandstone, and its layout includes granaries, water reserves, 
    and residential quarters for army men. The fort played a crucial role in protecting the region and 
    its valuable treasures.
