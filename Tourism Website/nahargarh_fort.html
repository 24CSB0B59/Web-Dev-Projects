Nahargarh Fort

            
             Nahargarh Fort, perched on the rugged ridge of the Aravalli Hills, overlooks the Pink City
             of Jaipur in Rajasthan, India. The fort was originally named Sudarshangarh, but it later 
             became known as Nahargarh, which means 'abode of tigers'. 

             Built in 1734 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, the founder of Jaipur, the fort was primarily 
             constructed as a retreat. Over the years, Nahargarh Fort has been a witness to many historical 
             events, including the treaties with the Marathas in the 18th century.

             Nahargarh Fort offers a panoramic view of Jaipur and provides a stunning backdrop when viewed 
             from the city. Its scenic location and historical significance make it a popular tourist destination.

Architecture and Features

            
    The architecture of Nahargarh Fort is a striking blend of Indian and European styles. Inside 
    the fort are several structures, including palaces, temples, and other buildings, which have 
    been well-preserved over the centuries.

    Some of the key features of Nahargarh Fort include:

    - *Madhavendra Bhawan*: This palace complex includes suites for the king and his queens, each with 
      bedroom, lobby, kitchen, and toilet. It is adorned with intricate frescoes and stucco designs.
    - *Diwan-i-Aam*: An open-air enclosure where the king held public meetings and addressed grievances.
    - *Nahargarh Biological Park*: Located at the foot of the fort, it houses a variety of flora and fauna, 
      including Asiatic lions, Bengal tigers, and Indian leopards.
    - *Temples*: There are several temples within the fort, the most notable being the temple dedicated to 
      the deity of Jaipur rulers.
    - *Water Tanks*: The fort has an impressive water harvesting system with tanks that collect and store rainwater.
